Why is Computer Science so important?

Of all majors, Computer Science majors have the highest average starting salary.
Over the next ten years, over 1,000,000 Computer Science jobs worth $500 billion will be unfilled.
Students are 16% more likely to recieve full-time jobs out of college with a Computer Science major.

What we do in Computer Science Club

Develop

Computer Science Club has always been on the forefront of technology. From programming our nine autonomous drones to 3D scanning and 3D printing or creating virtual reality simulators and building IoT motion controlled appliances.

Learn

We take the time to educate members of our club to further deepen their skills and interest in Computer Science and provide them with the skills, knowledge, and experience they need to be safe and succeed both online and in person.

Compete

The Computer Science Club competes in all day hacking competitions, Computer Science quiz bowls, programming challenges, an IT Innovation Cup, and ranks highly in various national and global online hacking CTF competitions.

What you can learn

Web Development

Learn to create and design websites by yourself without the use of any software. Learn the basic languages of web development including HTML, CSS, and JavaScript and make your websites sleek and smooth with common JavaScript frameworks like Angular.js, and jQuery. Construct elaborate back-end services using the biggest programming languages like Java, Python, and PHP, and Ruby.

Cybersecurity

Learn the skills and tactics of cyberwarfare. Between offensive and defensive security, utilize powerful tools like Kali Linux and Wireshark to be able to penetrate dummy networks, and recognize exploitations in yours. Master skills such as SQL Injection, Cross-Site Script Attacks, DDoS, and MD5 encryption hashes to understand, recognize, and defend against common exploitations and their effects.

Programming

Learn the art and power of programming from simple web functions in JavaScript to advanced algorithms and programs in Java, Python, and C++. Study the theory and history of programming to better understand the syntactic and semantic differences between advanced languages. Understand how and why programs compile and run, and how you can ensure your programs run as efficiently and as effectively as possible.

Hardware Engineering

Learn the components and the respective functions on the inside of the computers around you. Take apart laptops, phones, and desktops to learn how each part interacts with others. Grasp advanced concepts of the fundemental design of computers and how a small circuit utilizes transistors and logic gates to provide computers with their basic functions. Learn to recognize and repair common hardware issues.

Game Design

Take a step beyond playing your favorite games by learning how they work and possible causes for common gaming issues. Use your prior knowledge of gaming to develop your own game without the need for extensive knowledge of programming languages. Embark on a project using Unity or Unreal Engine that you can succesfully compile and distribute to all of your friends without paying a dime.

Network Systems

Learn the essential functions and capabilities of network systems. Learn to maintain and construct your own network infrastructure and understand the effort needed to successfully and safely maintain a server. Utilize the skills you learn to be able to conceptualize the relationships established over networks, as well as the common exploitations and how to protect against powerful tools aimed to penetrate or smash it.

Cryptography

Learn the practices and theories of cryptography that originated long before computer systems. Understand the algorithms of basic cryptographic functions such as the ceasar cipher, and hexadecimal encoding. Then move on to more advanced cryptographic methods such as Base64 encoding and finally graduate with a knowledge of MD5 hashing, and the most complex cryptographics like AES and RSA encryption.

Database Management

Learn the often overlooked skills of database management. Discover the functions of different database serices and be able to establish the most efficient and effective form of database relationship for specific services. Be able to create databases for a variety of uses found in almost any career field from financial services to web user information storage. Learn how to safely and securely communicate and interact with a database.
