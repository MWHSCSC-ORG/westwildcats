Computer Science Club Constitution

Computer Science Club Constitution


PREAMBLE

Section 1: Name. The name of this organization shall be the Millard West Computer Science Club, hereafter referred to as the CSC.

Section 2: Definitions

a. Membership. Members of the Computer Science Club are those students in regular attendance of the CSC meetings. Membership may also be conferred on students unable to attend club dates but who demonstrate outside effort to participate in the club and club activities. Students are members for the duration of their involvement. Membership may be revoked if adherence to the Millard West student handbook is violated, students violate other district policies or if a student creates a negative atmosphere through behavior unbecoming a member as determined by the CSC sponsor.

b. Frame of Reference. All times cited in this Constitution refer to times in which the Millard West High School is in session during the fall and spring semesters.

Section 2: Purpose. The purpose of this organization shall be to:

  • Compete in Computer Science Competitions throughout the frame of reference of the CSC;

  • Enhance the learning environment of Millard West High School;

  • Promote Computer Science and technology education within Millard West and the community;

  • Discuss, to act upon, and to communicate the opinion of the CSC on matters of interest to the Administration;

ARTICLE I: Membership

Section 1: Membership.

a. Eligibility. All Members must have a minimum 2.0 GPA. The Member must not be on conduct probation at the Millard West, and may not be a district employee. If at any time a member fails to meet these requirements, or those as defined by district participation guidelines, they shall be considered removed from the CSC.

b. Attendance. Failure to attend regular meetings or participate in CSC activities shall be grounds for expulsion from the CSC for the semester of expulsion.

c. Minimum Membership. This student organization shall consist of at least four currently enrolled student members.

Section 2: Duties and Powers. Each Member of the CSC may submit club business as approved by the Sponsor.

a. The Members shall consider and vote upon all business pertaining to required action of significance.

b. The Members may override all Presidential vetoes, pursuant to Article II, Section 2 of this Constitution.

c. The Members shall consider and vote upon all appointments.

d. The Members shall assume all duties, necessary to the functioning of the CSC not already delegated to another body or official.

ARTICLE II: CSC BUSINESS

Section 1: Meetings of the Members. Meetings of the Members shall be chaired by the CSC Vice-President. Should the Vice-President be temporarily absent then the Association Secretary shall be the Chair pro-tempore. In the event that both the Vice-President and the Secretary are temporarily absent, then the President shall chair the meeting until a Chairpro-temporeis chosen.

a. Rights of Debate in Meetings. All Members may speak, make and second motions, and vote on all CSC business, unless otherwise restricted from doing so. The President, is only permitted to vote on business in the case of a tie.

b. Voting. Members, or their recognized proxies, must be present to vote. No member shall cast more than one vote per balloting. In the event of a tie, the President shall cast the deciding vote.

c. Quorum. Quorum for meetings shall consist of a simple majority of the Members.

d. Parliamentary Authority. The latest edition of Robert’s Rules of Order (newly revised) shall be the authority on any parliamentary questions. If there is conflicting or unclear information in Robert’s Rules of Order, the President shall make the final decision.

e. Regular Sessions. Regular sessions shall be set by the Executive Board with at least twenty four (24) hours prior notice. Regular sessions shall be held at least every week during the fall and spring semesters unless the regular session falls on a recognized student holiday. The CSC shall not meet during spring break or finals week, unless a majority of the CSC deems it necessary.

f. Special Sessions. Special sessions of the CSC may be called by the Sponsor should it be deemed important or necessary by the sponsor and/or executive board.

Section 2: Legislation. Upon Member approval by the margin specified in this Constitution or Parliamentary Authority, legislation shall be forwarded to the President for approval or veto. If any two documents are found to be in conflict on this matter, the margin defined in the highest precedence document shall be the margin used. The order of precedence shall be the following: Constitution, and parliamentary authority.

a. Passage of Legislation. Should the President approve the legislation or fail to veto it prior to the regular session immediately following original passage, the legislation shall be considered passed and enacted appropriately.

b. Presidential Veto. Vetoed legislation shall not be considered passed and shall not be executed. The President shall, at the regular session immediately following original passage, notify the Members that the legislation has been vetoed. Vetoed legislation must appear on the agenda by the second regular session following original passage. Should the President fail to follow correctly any of the aforementioned procedures, the legislation shall be considered passed and shall be enacted appropriately.

c. Veto Override. When vetoed legislation appears on the agenda, the Members may consider overriding the Presidential Veto. With a two thirds (2/3) majority vote the Members may override Presidential Vetoes. For legislation that originally required a two thirds (2/3) majority vote for passage, a three fourths (3/4) majority vote shall be required to override a Presidential Veto.

d. Exemption from Veto. Legislation dealing with the impeachment or removal of Officers, or amending this Constitution, is not subject to veto.

Section 3: Officers

a. Should a permanent vacancy occur in the office of the President, the Vice-President shall assume the office of the President.

b. Should a permanent vacancy occur in the office of the Vice-President, the Treasurer or the Secretary, then the President shall appoint a replacement within seven (7) days.

c. Should permanent vacancies occur simultaneously in the offices of the President and Vice-President, then a special election can be held.

Section 4: Removal of Officers

a. Impeachment of Elected Officers. Valid grounds for impeachment of elected officers shall be failure to discharge the duties of office, and/or conduct unbecoming an CSC Officer.

b. Procedure

1. Impeachment proceedings shall be initiated by the Sponsor and is non-negotiable. No notice may be required to the Membership should such occurrence take place.

2. A call for impeachment may be made by the Membership and can be voted on. Should this occur the final decision is to be made by the Sponsor. Only votes of 3/4 of the Membership will be adequate grounds for impeachment.

ARTICLE III: ASSOCIATION OFFICERS

Section 1: Officers. The Officers of the Association shall be the President, Vice-President, Secretary, Treasurer.. All Officers shall report to the Membership upon request and take on any additional duties as specified in this Constitution, or in parliamentary authority. If at any time an officer fails to meet the requirements set forth below, he or she shall be considered removed from his or her office.

a. Elected Officers. The President, Vice-President, Secretary and Treasurer shall be elected annually by the Membership of the Association. They shall assume the duties of their office at the first regular meeting of the CSC after their election. The President and Vice-President shall be elected as a single item on the ballot. The Secretary and the Treasurer shall each be elected separately.

b. Eligibility of Elected Officers. Elected Officers must have a minimum 2.0 cumulative GPA, cannot be on conduct probation at Millard West High School, and cannot be a school employee.

Section 2: Duties and Powers of Association Officers.

a. The President shall:

1. Verify quorum and attendance at meetings of the Executive Board;
2. Report to the Members on all Executive Board actions;
3. Serve as the CSC representative;
4. Appoint alternate or additional representatives with Executive Board approval;
5. Appoint Association and Special Committee chairs;
6. Appoint replacements for unfilled executive offices;
7. Not have the power to submit legislation to the Association Senate;
8. Not have the right to vote, make, or second motions;
9. In good faith, and to the best of his or her ability, enact all duly passed legislation;
10. Direct action to the CSC and Special Committees; and
11. Preside over meetings of the Membership when the Vice-President, Secretary, and Treasurer are unable to do so.

b. The Vice-President shall:

1. Set agendas for, call, verify quorum and attendance for, and preside over meetings;
2. In the event of a temporary absence of the President, assume the duties of the President, for the duration of the President’s absence;


c. The Secretary shall:


1. Keep a record of all organization activities;
2. Submit material to the administration;
3. Serve as a contact for all club business not handled by the Sponsor; and,
4. Chair meetings when the Vice-President is unable to do so.

d. The Treasurer shall:

1. Keep all financial records of the CSC;
2. Submit a balanced budget for Membership upon request; and,
3. Preside over meetings when the Vice-President and Secretary are unable to do so.

Section 3: Duties and Powers of Appointed Officers. The Appointed Officers shall be created at the behest of the Sponsor or executive board.

a. Appointed Officers shall:

1. Perform special project roles as determined by the executive board and Sponsor.

b. The Election Commissioner shall:

1. Coordinate and oversee all elections of the Association.

ARTICLE IV: Sponsors

Section 1: A Sponsor shall be an employee of Millard Public Schools.

Section 2: The Sponsor shall be appointed as long as the club is active.

Section 3: A Sponsor shall have the same rights of debate as a voting member, but shall not have the right to vote on motions of the body.

Section 4: This organization shall have one Primary Sponsor. Only the Primary Sponsor may approve expenditures of funds.

Section 5: The Sponsor has final authority over all club business and decisions.

ARTICLE V: FINANCE

Section 1: Funding of the CSC. Dues may be collected should dues be deemed necessary to conduct regular business, but must first be approved by the Sponsor and may only be done at the beginning of a semester.

Section 2: The CSC may execute any money-making projects which do not violate the provisions of this Constitution or any applicable state or federal law, and are approved by the appropriate Millard Public School agencies.

Section 3: The funds of the Association shall be handled by the Financial Office of Millard West, regardless of source.

Section 4: The CSC shall operate within its budget. No revision subsequently made to the budget shall place the proposed expenditures of the CSC in excess of its current financial resources.

ARTICLE VI: CONSTITUTIONAL AMENDMENTS

Section 1: Amendments to this Constitution shall require Prior Notice and a two thirds (2/3) vote of the Members in addition to ratification, as described below. Constitutional Amendments shall not be subject to Presidential Veto but are subject to Sponsor Veto.

Section 2: All amendments to this Constitution must be approved by the Sponsor. Constitutional amendments not approved by the Sponsor shall be considered null and void.

ARTICLE VII: DISTRIBUTION OF FINANCES AND PROPER BUSINESS AUTHORITY

Section 1: The Club shall operate according to the rules and guidelines of a non-profit entity. No part of the organization’s net earnings will inure to the benefit of individuals. The CSC must establish that it will not be organized or operated for the benefit of private interests, such as the creator or the creator’s family, shareholders of the organization, other designated individuals, or persons controlled directly or indirectly by such private interests. The CSC shall be authorized and empowered to pay reasonable compensation for services rendered and to make payments and distributions in furtherance of the purpose set forth in this document.

ARTICLE VIII: Choosing Competitors for Club Competitions

Section 1: Competitors for competitions shall be jointly determined by the Sponsor and executive board.

Section 2: Purposes for determination:

  1. Level of computer science currently taken at Millard West;

  2. Extraordinary aptitude as determined by the Sponsor and executive board;

  3. Competitive selection processes as facilitated by club competitions;

  4. Club dedication and work ethic as determined by attendance and observations of the Sponsor and executive board.

ARTICLE IX: PROCESS FOR DISSOLUTION

Section 1: Upon the dissolution of the CSC the officers and Sponsor shall after paying or making provisions for the payment of all liabilities of the CSC, dispose of all the assets of the CSC in the form of a donation to Millard West Computer Science courses. Under Section 501C of the Internal Revenue Code of 1986 (or the corresponding provision of any future United States Internal Revenue Law), as the officers and advisors shall determine. Any such assets not disposed of shall be disposed of by Millard Public Schools.

ARTICLE X: NON-DISCRIMINATION

Section 1: The Computer Science Club shall not discriminate in the selection of members or appointments when discrimination is defined as denying individuals membership or appointment on the basis of gender, age, disability, race, color, religion, marital status, veteran’s status, national or ethnic origin, gender identity or expression, place of residence, or sexual orientation.

ARTICLE XI: CONSTITUTIONAL AUTHORITY

Section 1: This Constitution is the final authority in all CSC business.

ARTICLE XII: PERIODIC REVISION OF THE CONSTITUTION

Section 1: This Constitution shall be revised periodically at the discretion of the Sponsor.

ARTICLE XIII: EFFECTIVE DATE

Section 1: This Constitution shall go into effect August 29, 2014.
